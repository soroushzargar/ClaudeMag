1. Optimal Decision-Making Based on Prediction Sets [Oral — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Tao Wang, Edgar Dobriban
   Affiliation: University of Pennsylvania (Wharton Statistics and Data Science)
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Oral (Jul 7, 2026, 2:15-2:30 PM, Grand Ballroom 101-105) | arXiv:2602.00989
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00989
   Summary: Prediction sets can wrap around any ML model to cover unknown test outcomes with a
   guaranteed probability, but it has been unclear how to use them optimally for downstream
   decisions. This paper introduces a decision-theoretic framework that minimizes worst-case
   expected loss subject to the coverage guarantee, characterizes the minimax-optimal policy for
   a fixed prediction set, and derives the optimal set construction itself. The resulting
   algorithm, Risk-Optimal Conformal Prediction (ROCP), targets these risk-minimizing sets while
   retaining finite-sample, distribution-free marginal coverage, consistently reducing worst-case
   risk and critical mistake rates relative to risk-averse max-min and best-response baselines.

2. Online Conformal Prediction via Universal Portfolio Algorithms [Spotlight — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Tuo Liu, Edgar Dobriban, Francesco Orabona
   Affiliation: University of Pennsylvania, Boston University
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Spotlight | arXiv:2602.03168
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03168
   Summary: Online conformal prediction (OCP) seeks intervals with long-run 1-alpha coverage on
   arbitrary, possibly adversarial data streams, but existing methods require manual learning-rate
   tuning and bespoke regret analyses. The authors develop a general regret-to-coverage theory for
   interval-valued OCP based on the (1-alpha)-pinball loss, showing that controlling "linearized
   regret" implies coverage bounds for any online algorithm via a black-box reduction using the
   Fenchel conjugate of the regret bound. This yields UP-OCP, a parameter-free method that reduces
   OCP to a two-asset portfolio-selection problem and solves it with universal portfolio algorithms,
   eliminating the learning-rate tuning that plagued prior online conformal methods.

3. Conf-Gen: Conformal Uncertainty Quantification for Generative Models [Poster — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Gabriel Loaiza-Ganem, Kevin Zhang, Wei Cui, Marc Law, Kin Kwan Leung
   Affiliation: Layer 6 AI (TD Bank Group)
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Poster #61800 (Jul 8, 2026, 10:30 AM-12:15 PM KST) | arXiv:2605.28920
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28920
   Summary: Conformal prediction and conformal risk control (CRC) give formal uncertainty
   guarantees for supervised models, but modern generative systems -- LLMs, image generators,
   conversational agents -- are unsupervised and don't fit the CRC mold. Conf-Gen adapts CRC to
   generative tasks by relaxing its theoretical assumptions, unifying and generalizing prior
   ad hoc attempts to apply conformal methods to LLMs. The framework is demonstrated on
   conformal guarantees that image generators produce non-memorized outputs, that conversational
   AI systems have asked enough clarifying questions before answering, and that AI agent outputs
   are correct -- extending conformal methodology into domains with no natural notion of a
   held-out label.

4. Calibrating Decision Robustness via Inverse Conformal Risk Control [Poster — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Wenbin Zhou, Shixiang Zhu
   Affiliation: Carnegie Mellon University
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Poster #61939 (Jul 9, 2026, 10:30 AM-12:15 PM KST) | arXiv:2510.07750
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07750
   Summary: Robust optimization guards decisions against uncertainty by hedging against worst-case
   scenarios within a conformal uncertainty set, but the robustness (coverage) level is usually
   fixed a priori and ad hoc, yielding either under-protection or wasteful conservatism. Inverse
   Conformal Risk Control (ICRC) flips the usual conformal recipe: instead of fixing coverage and
   measuring downstream regret, it provides distribution-free, finite-sample guarantees on both
   miscoverage and regret simultaneously, tracing out the miscoverage-regret Pareto frontier so
   practitioners can select a robustness level according to their own cost-risk preferences.

5. Provably Label-Efficient Conformal Prediction [Poster — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Andrew Ilyas, Joonhyuk Ko, Jingwu Tang, Zhiwei Steven Wu, Jiahao Zhang
   Affiliation: Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Carnegie Mellon University
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Poster #65796 (Jul 6, 2026, 6:30-8:15 PM PDT, Hall A #4516)
   URL: https://icml.cc/virtual/2026/poster/65796
   Summary: Conformal prediction gives finite-sample coverage guarantees but depends on a
   randomly sampled labeled calibration set, which is expensive to collect -- creating a tradeoff
   between upfront labeling cost and the downstream efficiency (prediction-set size) of the
   resulting conformal predictor. The authors formalize conformal prediction under costly,
   sequential label queries and design an online stopping rule that automatically balances
   calibration-set size against conformal efficiency while exactly preserving the coverage
   guarantee. They prove the expected total cost matches the best fixed calibration size in
   hindsight, and show the stopping rule cuts labeling cost by 41.4% +/- 2.3% experimentally.
