1. LLMs Get Lost In Multi-Turn Conversation [Outstanding Paper / Oral — ICLR 2026]
   Authors: Philippe Laban, Hiroaki Hayashi, Yingbo Zhou, Jennifer Neville
   Venue/ID: ICLR 2026 Outstanding Paper | arXiv:2505.06120
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.06120
   Summary: Investigates how LLM task performance deteriorates across extended multi-turn
   conversations, showing accuracy drops of roughly 39% compared to single-turn equivalents.
   The study reveals that models increasingly lose track of user intent and context as dialogue
   length grows, with implications for production-grade conversational AI systems.

2. Transformers are Inherently Succinct [Outstanding Paper / Oral — ICLR 2026]
   Authors: Pascal Bergsträßer, Ryan Cotterell, Anthony W. Lin
   Venue/ID: ICLR 2026 Outstanding Paper | arXiv:2510.19315
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.19315
   Summary: Establishes that fixed-precision transformers are exponentially more succinct than
   both linear temporal logic (LTL) and recurrent neural networks, and doubly exponentially
   more succinct than finite automata. As a corollary, verifying even simple properties of
   transformers is shown to be EXPSPACE-complete, grounding transformer expressivity in
   classical complexity theory.

3. Efficiently Reconstructing Dynamic Scenes One D4RT at a Time [Best Paper / Oral — CVPR 2026]
   Authors: Chuhan Zhang, Guillaume Le Moing, Skanda Koppula, Ignacio Rocco, Liliane Momeni,
   Junyu Xie, Shuyang Sun, Rahul Sukthankar, Joëlle K. Barral, Raia Hadsell,
   Zoubin Ghahramani, Andrew Zisserman, Junlin Zhang, Mehdi S. M. Sajjadi
   Affiliation: Google DeepMind, University College London, University of Oxford
   Venue/ID: CVPR 2026 Best Paper
   URL: https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2026/html/Zhang_Efficiently_Reconstructing_Dynamic_Scenes_One_D4RT_at_a_Time_CVPR_2026_paper.html
   Summary: Introduces D4RT, a unified feedforward transformer that jointly infers depth,

   benchmarks across a wide spectrum of 4D reconstruction tasks.

4. Maximum Likelihood Reinforcement Learning [Oral — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Fahim Tajwar, Guanning Zeng, Yueer Zhou, Yuda Song, Daman Arora, Yiding Jiang,
   Jeff Schneider, Russ Salakhutdinov, Haiwen Feng, Andrea Zanette
   Affiliation: Carnegie Mellon University
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Oral | arXiv:2602.02710
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02710
   Summary: Identifies that standard RL objectives in sampling-based setups (e.g. code
   generation, math) optimize only a lower-order approximation of the true likelihood over
   correct rollouts rather than maximizing it directly. Introduces MaxRL, a family of objectives
   that smoothly interpolates between REINFORCE and maximum likelihood, achieving stronger
   results on correctness-based tasks with binary outcome feedback.

5. RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist Policies [Spotlight — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Yinpei Dai, Hongze Fu, Jayjun Lee, Yuejiang Liu, Haoran Zhang, Jianing Yang,
   Chelsea Finn, Nima Fazeli, Joyce Chai
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Spotlight | arXiv:2603.04639
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04639
   Summary: Presents RoboMME, a large-scale standardized benchmark for evaluating memory in
   vision-language-action (VLA) models, comprising 16 manipulation tasks spanning temporal,
   spatial, object, and procedural memory categories. Also introduces 14 memory-augmented VLA

   far from solving long-horizon manipulation reliably.
