1. To Grok Grokking: Provable Grokking in Ridge Regression [Honorable Mention — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Mingyue Xu, Gal Vardi, Itay Safran
   Affiliation: Hebrew University of Jerusalem; Ben-Gurion University of the Negev
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Honorable Mention | arXiv:2601.19791
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19791
   Summary: Provides the first end-to-end mathematical proof of grokking — the phenomenon where
   a model overfits the training data early in training yet eventually transitions to near-perfect
   generalization — in the classical ridge regression setting. The authors prove that gradient
   descent with weight decay necessarily passes through three sequential phases (overfitting,
   persistent poor generalization, eventual generalization) and derive exact quantitative bounds
   on the grokking time as a function of every hyperparameter.

2. A Random Matrix Theory Perspective on the Consistency of Diffusion Models [Honorable Mention — ICML 2026]
   Authors: Binxu Wang, Jacob A. Zavatone-Veth, Cengiz Pehlevan
   Affiliation: Harvard Kempner Institute; Harvard Society of Fellows; Harvard SEAS
   Venue/ID: ICML 2026 Oral / Honorable Mention | arXiv:2602.02908
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02908
   Summary: Explains why diffusion models trained on different, non-overlapping subsets of a
   dataset produce strikingly similar images from the same random noise seed. The paper traces
   the consistency to a simple linear effect — shared Gaussian statistics across data splits
   already predict most of the generated output — and formalizes this through a random matrix
   theory (RMT) framework quantifying how finite dataset size reshapes the learned denoiser
   via a self-consistent noise-level renormalization σ² → κ(σ²).

3. VLM-AR3L: Vision-Language Models for Absolute and Relative Rewards in Reinforcement Learning [IJCAI 2026]
   Authors: Kuan-Chen Chen, Winston Chen, Wei-Fang Sun, Min-Chun Hu
   Affiliation: National Tsing Hua University; NVIDIA AI Technology Center (NVAITC)
   Venue/ID: IJCAI 2026 | arXiv:2607.00483
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00483
   Summary: Introduces a framework leveraging Vision-Language Models to provide dual reward
   signals for reinforcement learning without environment-specific reward engineering. An absolute
   reward model predicts scalar state-quality evaluations; a relative reward model compares
   consecutive visual observations to infer task progress or regression. Evaluating on open-world
   Minecraft tasks from MineDojo (Combat Spider, Milk Cow, Shear Sheep, Hunt Cow), VLM-AR3L
   consistently outperforms prior VLM-based reward learning baselines.

4. BiSCo-LLM: Lookup-Free Binary Spherical Coding for Extreme Low-Bit Large Language Model Compression [arXiv 2026]
   Authors: Yuantian Shao, Peisong Wang, Zhilei Liu, Chuangyi Li, Yuanteng Chen,
   Pengcheng Xie, Yiwu Yao, Zhihui Wei, Jian Cheng
   Affiliation: Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences; University of Chinese Academy of Sciences; Huawei Technologies
   Venue/ID: arXiv:2607.08643
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08643
   Summary: Proposes binary spherical coding (BSCo) — a lookup-table-free quantization scheme
   for compressing LLM weights to extreme low-bit representations. By mapping weight vectors
   onto binary codes that lie on the surface of a high-dimensional sphere, the method achieves
   near-1-bit compression while maintaining competitive perplexity on language benchmarks.
   The elimination of lookup tables reduces decoding overhead and makes on-device deployment
   of large models substantially more practical.

5. Latent Memory Palace: Reasoning for Control as Autoregressive Variational Inference [arXiv 2026]
   Authors: Chuning Zhu, Eva Xu, Jose Barreiros, Krishnan Srinivasan, Paarth Shah, Abhishek Gupta
   Affiliation: Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering, University of Washington
   Venue/ID: arXiv:2607.08724
   URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08724
   Summary: Transfers adaptive, depth-variable reasoning from language models to continuous
   control policies by casting deliberation as autoregressive variational inference over a
   structured latent state space. The agent learns when to think longer (allocating more latent
   computation steps) versus acting immediately, enabling flexible depth-of-reasoning allocation
   across physical control tasks without hand-designed planning modules.
